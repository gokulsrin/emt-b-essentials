## Front

Compare <b>asthma</b>, <b>COPD exacerbation</b>, and <b>CHF / pulmonary edema</b> — typical history, lung sounds, and key field treatment.

## Back

| | <b>Asthma</b> | <b>COPD</b> | <b>CHF / pulmonary edema</b> |
|--|--|--|--|
| History | Younger, episodic; triggers (cold, exercise, allergens) | Smoker, gradual, "always short of breath" | Cardiac history, sudden onset, often nocturnal, leg edema, JVD |
| Sounds | Expiratory wheezing | Wheezing + diminished breath sounds | Crackles/rales (wet), pink frothy sputum |
| Treatment | O₂, position of comfort, albuterol | O₂ titrated to SpO₂ 88–92%, albuterol | Sit upright legs dangling, high-flow O₂, CPAP if available, rapid transport |

## Extra

In COPD, avoid blasting 100% O₂ if SpO₂ is already adequate — target ~88–92% to avoid blunting hypoxic drive. In CHF, do not lay the patient flat; upright with legs dependent reduces venous return. Diuretics and nitro are outside EMT-B scope unless assisting the patient's own prescription.
