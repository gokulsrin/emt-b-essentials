## Text

Single-rescuer <b>BVM</b> mask seal uses the <u>E-C clamp</u>: thumb and index finger form a {{c1::"C"::letter}} pressing the mask down onto the face, while the third through fifth fingers form an {{c1::"E"::letter}} lifting the mandible up into the mask. If you cannot maintain seal or chest rise is inadequate, switch to {{c2::two-rescuer BVM::escalation}} — one provider seals the mask with both hands, the other squeezes the bag.

## Extra

Inadequate chest rise is almost always a seal problem, not a bag problem. Two-rescuer BVM produces consistently better tidal volumes than one-rescuer technique and is preferred whenever staffing allows. Squeeze the bag over ~1 second until visible chest rise — over-aggressive squeezing causes gastric insufflation, vomiting, and aspiration.
