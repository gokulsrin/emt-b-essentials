## Text

<b>Low EtCO₂</b> (&lt;35 mmHg) suggests {{c1::hyperventilation, shock, or PE::causes}}; <b>high EtCO₂</b> (&gt;45 mmHg) suggests {{c2::hypoventilation or respiratory failure::causes}}.

## Extra

In cardiac arrest, a sudden rise in EtCO₂ often signals ROSC. Trending EtCO₂ guides BVM rate — slow bagging if EtCO₂ is rising above normal.
