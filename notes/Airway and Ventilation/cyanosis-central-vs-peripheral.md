## Text

<b>Central cyanosis</b> (lips, tongue) indicates {{c1::systemic hypoxemia::clinical meaning}}; <b>peripheral cyanosis</b> (nail beds) may indicate only {{c2::local hypoperfusion::clinical meaning}} with normal SpO₂.

## Extra

Central cyanosis is more clinically significant — it reflects low arterial O₂ saturation. Peripheral cyanosis can occur in cold environments or with vasoconstriction even when oxygenation is adequate.
