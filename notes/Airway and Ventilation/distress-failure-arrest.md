## Front

Distinguish <b>respiratory distress</b>, <b>respiratory failure</b>, and <b>respiratory arrest</b> — and the right intervention for each.

## Back

<b>Respiratory distress</b> — patient is compensating: ↑ rate, ↑ work of breathing, accessory muscles; mental status, skin signs, and SpO₂ still acceptable.<br>
Treat: position of comfort, supplemental O₂ (NC or NRB titrated to SpO₂ ≥ 94%).

<b>Respiratory failure</b> — compensation breaking down: rate too slow or too fast and shallow, decreased LOC, cyanosis, falling SpO₂, exhaustion, altered mental status.<br>
Treat: assist ventilations with BVM at 15 L/min O₂ — even if the patient is still breathing.

<b>Respiratory arrest</b> — no breathing, no air movement.<br>
Treat: immediate BVM ventilations; if no pulse, start CPR.

## Extra

Triggers to upgrade from NRB to BVM: declining LOC, SpO₂ falling despite high-flow O₂, RR < 8 or > 30 with poor depth, "quiet chest" in asthma.
