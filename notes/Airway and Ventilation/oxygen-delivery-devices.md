## Front

Compare the four main <b>oxygen delivery devices</b>: flow rate, FiO₂, and when to use each.

## Back

| Device | Flow (L/min) | FiO₂ | Use when |
|--------|--------------|------|----------|
| <b>Nasal cannula</b> | 1–6 | ~24–44% | Mild distress, SpO₂ slightly low, comfort |
| <b>Simple face mask</b> | 6–10 | ~40–60% | Moderate distress; flow ≥ 6 to flush CO₂ |
| <b>Non-rebreather (NRB)</b> | 10–15 | ~80–95% | Significant distress, breathing adequate in rate/depth |
| <b>BVM with O₂ reservoir</b> | 15 | ~100% | Inadequate breathing or apnea; rescuer ventilates patient |

## Extra

NRB is for a patient who is breathing adequately but hypoxic. The moment breathing becomes inadequate (rate too slow, depth too shallow, worsening LOC), switch to BVM. A simple mask below 6 L/min causes CO₂ rebreathing.
