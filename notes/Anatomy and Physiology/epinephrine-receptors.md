## Text

<b>Epinephrine</b> is released from the {{c1::adrenal medulla::source}} and acts on three receptor types:
{{c2::Beta-1}} (heart → ↑ HR and ↑ contractility), {{c2::Beta-2}} (lungs/vessels → bronchodilation), {{c2::Alpha-1}} (vessels → peripheral vasoconstriction).

This triple action makes epi the drug of choice for {{c3::anaphylaxis::indication}}.

## Extra

Albuterol is selective for beta-2 only — that is why it bronchodilates with much less tachycardia than epinephrine. A patient with isolated bronchospasm (asthma) gets albuterol; a patient with full anaphylaxis gets epinephrine first.
