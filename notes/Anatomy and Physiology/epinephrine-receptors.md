## Text

<b>Epinephrine</b> is released from the {{c1::adrenal medulla::source}} and acts on three receptor types:
{{c2::Beta-1}}, {{c2::Beta-2}}, {{c2::Alpha-1}}.

This triple action makes epi the drug of choice for {{c3::anaphylaxis::indication}}.

## Extra

Beta-1: heart → ↑ HR and contractility. Beta-2: lungs/vessels → bronchodilation. Alpha-1: vessels → peripheral vasoconstriction. Albuterol is selective for beta-2 only — bronchodilates with much less tachycardia. A patient with full anaphylaxis gets epinephrine; isolated bronchospasm (asthma) gets albuterol.
