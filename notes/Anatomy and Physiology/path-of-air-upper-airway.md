## Text

Inhaled air enters via the {{c1::mouth or nose::entry points}}, passes through the
{{c1::pharynx::structure}} (nasopharynx → oropharynx → laryngopharynx), then through the
{{c1::larynx::structure}} (vocal cords) — the boundary between upper and lower airway.

## Extra

The pharynx is divided into three regions by level: nasopharynx (behind the nose), oropharynx (behind the mouth), laryngopharynx (above the larynx). The larynx marks where the upper airway ends and the lower airway begins.
