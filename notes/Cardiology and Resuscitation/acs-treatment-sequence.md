## Text

<b>EMT field care sequence</b> for suspected <b>ACS</b>:

1. {{c1::Position of comfort}} (usually semi-Fowler's); calm patient, limit exertion
2. {{c1::Oxygen only if SpO₂ < 94%}} or signs of distress — routine high-flow O₂ in normoxic ACS not recommended
3. {{c1::Aspirin 324 mg (4 × 81 mg) chewed}} if no contraindication
4. {{c1::Assist with prescribed nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL}} if SBP > 100 mmHg, no PDE-5 inhibitor in last 24–72 h, no right-sided MI signs
5. {{c1::12-lead ECG}} if available; rapid transport to PCI-capable hospital
6. {{c1::Reassess vitals, pain, mental status every 5 minutes}}

## Extra

The old "MONA" mnemonic (Morphine, Oxygen, Nitro, Aspirin) is outdated as a
sequence — it implies all four are routine. Modern care: ASA early, nitro per protocol,
O₂ only if hypoxic, morphine is an in-hospital decision.
Nitro contraindications: SBP under 100, PDE-5 inhibitor use (sildenafil, tadalafil, vardenafil),
suspected right-sided MI (inferior ST elevation with hemodynamic compromise).
