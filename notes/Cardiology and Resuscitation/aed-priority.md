## Front

In a witnessed adult cardiac arrest, what is your <b>first priority</b> once an AED arrives — and where do the pads go?

## Back

<b>Priority:</b> apply the <b>AED</b> and deliver a shock as soon as possible if a shockable rhythm is present. <u>Early defibrillation</u> is the single biggest determinant of survival in <b>V-fib / pulseless V-tach</b>. Continue chest compressions until the AED says "analyzing — clear."

<b>Standard pad placement (anterolateral):</b>
- Right pad: upper right chest, below the clavicle, right of the sternum
- Left pad: left mid-axillary line, lateral to the left nipple, lower ribs

After a shock: immediately resume compressions for <b>2 minutes</b>, then re-analyze.

## Extra

Anterior–posterior placement (one between the nipples, one between the shoulder blades)
is an acceptable alternative — useful in small adults and children with adult pads.
Don't waste time choosing — the goal is "shocks flow."
