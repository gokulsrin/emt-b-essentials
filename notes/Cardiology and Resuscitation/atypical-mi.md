## Front

Which patients are most likely to present with <b>atypical MI</b> symptoms — and what should you look for instead of <u>"crushing chest pain"</u>?

## Back

<b>Highest risk for atypical presentations:</b>
- <b>Women</b>
- <b>Elderly</b>
- <b>Diabetics</b> (neuropathy blunts visceral pain)

<b>Atypical presentations to recognize:</b>
- Sudden weakness, fatigue, or malaise — <u>"I just feel awful"</u>
- Dyspnea without chest pain
- Syncope or near-syncope
- Nausea, vomiting, or epigastric / "indigestion" pain
- New-onset confusion (especially elderly)
- Pain in the jaw, neck, back, or either arm (not just left)
- Diaphoresis with no clear cause

## Extra

A stoic patient calling EMS for "indigestion" or "I don't feel right" with
no other findings is a classic atypical MI miss. When in doubt, treat as ACS:
12-lead ECG, ASA if appropriate, rapid transport, full reassessment.
