## Front

What is <b>cardiogenic shock</b> and how do you recognize and treat it in the field?

## Back

<b>Definition:</b> the heart fails as a pump — usually after a large MI — so it cannot generate adequate cardiac output despite normal volume.

<b>Recognition:</b>
- Hypotension (SBP < 90)
- Cool, clammy, pale skin (compensatory vasoconstriction)
- Tachycardia (sometimes bradycardia if AV block)
- Pulmonary edema findings: crackles, dyspnea, <u>JVD</u>, low SpO₂
- AMS, weak/thready peripheral pulses

<b>Treatment:</b>
- High-flow O₂ titrated to SpO₂ ≥ 94%; assist ventilations if inadequate
- Position based on respiratory effort — semi-Fowler's / upright if pulmonary edema; supine if no edema and BP needs support
- Do not lay flat with legs elevated if pulmonary edema is present
- Rapid transport to a PCI-capable hospital; ALS intercept

## Extra

Distinguish cardiogenic from hypovolemic shock by lung sounds and JVD:
cardiogenic has wet lungs and JVD; hypovolemic has clear lungs and flat neck veins.
Both present with pallor, clammy skin, and low BP.
