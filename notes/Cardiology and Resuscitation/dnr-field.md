## Text

A valid <b>DNR</b> must be {{c1::signed and dated by the physician}}, {{c1::patient-specific (not a hospital chart copy)}}, and {{c1::physically present or verifiable on scene}}. If any element is missing or unclear, the EMT default is to {{c2::begin resuscitation::default action}} — you can always stop once medical direction confirms validity.

## Extra

State-specific forms (POLST, MOLST, OOH-DNR bracelet/wallet card) satisfy the requirement when local protocol recognizes them. Verbal claims from family ("she didn't want this") without paperwork do not constitute a valid DNR. Document the order's source and decision rationale on the PCR; contact medical direction for any gray area.
