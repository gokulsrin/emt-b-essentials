## Text

<b>Infant CPR</b> compression technique: {{c1::two fingers::single rescuer}} or {{c1::two-thumb encircling hands::two rescuers}}.<br>
<b>Infant</b> pulse check site: {{c2::brachial artery::site}}.

## Extra

The two-thumb encircling technique allows the hands to squeeze the chest from all sides,
generating higher systolic pressure and coronary perfusion pressure than two-finger technique.
The brachial pulse is used because the infant neck is too short and fat to reliably palpate the carotid.
