## Front

How do you distinguish a <b>mild/moderate allergic reaction</b> from <b>anaphylaxis</b> in the field?

## Back

<b>Mild/moderate allergic reaction:</b>
- Limited to skin and mild GI: localized hives, itching, flushing, mild nausea
- No airway involvement, no respiratory distress, no hypotension
- Treatment: monitor, antihistamines per protocol, transport for observation

<b>Anaphylaxis:</b>
- Any airway/respiratory compromise (wheeze, stridor, swelling) OR any cardiovascular compromise (hypotension, AMS, syncope)
- Often (but not always) hives + GI symptoms in addition
- Treatment: immediate epinephrine IM, oxygen, transport

## Extra

The bar for epinephrine is intentionally low: any airway or BP involvement on top of allergic exposure — give it. Hives alone do not need epi; hives + difficulty breathing absolutely do.
