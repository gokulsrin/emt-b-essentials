## Text

<b>DKA</b> classic signs: <u>fruity (acetone) breath</u>, {{c1::Kussmaul respirations::breathing pattern}} (deep, rapid, labored), and {{c2::polyuria/polydipsia::symptoms}}; blood glucose typically &gt;250 mg/dL.

## Extra

Kussmaul respirations are the body's attempt to blow off CO₂ and compensate for metabolic acidosis. DKA most often occurs in type 1 diabetics with insulin omission, infection, or new diagnosis. Field treatment: O₂, IV fluids (ALS), transport.
