## Text

<b>Lower GI bleed</b> presents as {{c1::hematochezia::bright red rectal blood}}, indicating the blood source is distal to the {{c2::splenic flexure::landmark}}.

## Extra

Hematochezia can also occur with a massive upper GI bleed that transits rapidly. Causes include diverticulosis, hemorrhoids, colorectal cancer, and ischemic colitis. Treat for shock; transport.
