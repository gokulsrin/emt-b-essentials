## Text

<b>HHS</b> (hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state): extreme hyperglycemia ({{c1::&gt;600 mg/dL::glucose level}}), profound {{c2::dehydration::finding}}, and AMS — occurs in {{c3::elderly type 2 diabetics::population}}.

## Extra

HHS lacks the ketoacidosis of DKA (no fruity breath, no Kussmaul respirations) because residual insulin prevents ketone production. Dehydration is massive — patients can be liters behind. Mortality is higher than DKA; rapid transport and ALS for IV fluids.
