## Front

Compare <b>hypoglycemia</b> vs <b>hyperglycemia (DKA / HHS)</b> — onset, key signs, and field treatment.

## Back

| | Hypoglycemia | Hyperglycemia (DKA / HHS) |
|--|--|--|
| Onset | Sudden (minutes) | Gradual (hours-to-days) |
| Skin | Cool, pale, clammy | Warm, dry, dehydrated |
| Mental status | Anxious, confused, combative, seizure | Increasing lethargy, confusion |
| Breathing | Normal or rapid | Kussmaul (deep, fast) |
| Breath odor | Normal | Fruity / acetone (DKA) |
| Pulse | Tachycardic, weak | Tachycardic, weak; hypotensive in HHS |
| Glucose | &lt; 60–70 mg/dL | &gt; 250 mg/dL |
| Field tx | Oral glucose (15 g) if alert + can swallow; otherwise ALS for IV dextrose | O₂, IV fluids per ALS, transport |

## Extra

If you cannot distinguish and the patient is alert with intact swallow, oral glucose will not meaningfully harm a hyperglycemic patient — but it can save a hypoglycemic patient's life.
