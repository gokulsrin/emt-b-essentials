## Text

Any supine third-trimester patient should be positioned in {{c1::left lateral recumbent (left-side-down) tilt::position}} to relieve {{c2::aortocaval compression::mechanism}} by the gravid uterus.

## Extra

The gravid uterus compresses the inferior vena cava and aorta when the patient is supine, dramatically reducing cardiac output and causing maternal syncope and fetal distress. Manual left uterine displacement is an alternative if full lateral positioning is not possible.
