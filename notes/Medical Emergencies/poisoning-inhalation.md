## Text

<b>Inhalation poisoning</b> (gases, smoke, fumes, CO): {{c1::remove the patient from the source first::scene safety action}}, then give high-flow O₂ via NRB {{c2::regardless of pulse oximetry::qualifier}} because CO {{c3::falsely elevates SpO₂::effect on SpO₂}}.

## Extra

Pulse oximetry cannot distinguish oxyhemoglobin from carboxyhemoglobin — a CO patient can display 99% SpO₂ while severely poisoned. Only end-tidal CO or co-oximetry detects CO accurately. Rescuer safety is paramount — many EMS providers have been poisoned entering CO-filled spaces.
