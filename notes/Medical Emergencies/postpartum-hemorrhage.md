## Text

<b>Postpartum hemorrhage</b> management: perform {{c1::fundal massage::first intervention}}, place infant to {{c2::breastfeed::action}} to stimulate oxytocin, apply {{c3::perineal pads::local control}}, treat for shock, and transport.

## Extra

The most common cause of PPH is uterine atony (failure to contract after delivery). A normal uterine fundus should feel firm like a grapefruit at the navel. A boggy fundus indicates atony. Breastfeeding stimulates endogenous oxytocin, promoting uterine contraction.
