## Text

A <b>TIA</b> (transient ischemic attack) is defined by neurologic symptoms that {{c1::resolve within 24 hours::time course}} — treat the same as an acute stroke because {{c2::stroke risk is highest in the hours following a TIA::reason}}.

## Extra

TIA is a major warning sign: ~15% of strokes are preceded by a TIA, and 10–15% of TIA patients have a stroke within 90 days, with highest risk in the first 48 hours. Do not be reassured by symptom resolution; transport for urgent workup.
