## Text

<b>DOT placard</b> color → hazard class:<br>
<b>red</b> = {{c1::flammable}}, <b>orange</b> = {{c1::explosives}}, <b>yellow</b> = {{c1::oxidizer}}, <b>green</b> = {{c1::non-flammable gas}}, <b>blue</b> = {{c1::water-reactive (dangerous when wet)}}, <b>white</b> = {{c1::poison / inhalation hazard}}.

## Extra

Placards are diamond-shaped and displayed on transport vehicles. The 4-digit <b>UN number</b> on the placard identifies the specific substance — look it up in the <b>ERG</b> (Emergency Response Guidebook, orange book) for isolation distances and PPE. Approach hazmat scenes <u>uphill, upwind, upstream</u>. Black/white striped = corrosive (Class 8); yellow-and-white = radioactive (Class 7).
