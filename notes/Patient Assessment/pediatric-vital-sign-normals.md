## Text

Pediatric <b>heart rate</b> normals by age (bpm):

Newborn = {{c1::120–160::bpm}}, Infant (1–12 mo) = {{c1::100–140::bpm}}, Toddler (1–3 y) = {{c1::90–130::bpm}}, Child (6–12 y) = {{c1::70–110::bpm}}, Adolescent = {{c1::60–100::bpm}}.

## Extra

Tachycardia is the first sign of compensated shock in pediatrics — hypotension is a late, pre-arrest finding. By the time a child's BP drops, they are already crashing. A persistently fast heart rate in a child is your early warning.
