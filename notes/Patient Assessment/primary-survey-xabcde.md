## Text

<b>Primary assessment</b> — find and treat life threats in order (<b>XABCDE</b>):

{{c1::X — eXsanguinating hemorrhage control}}, {{c1::A — Airway}}, {{c1::B — Breathing}}, {{c1::C — Circulation}}, {{c1::D — Disability}}, {{c1::E — Exposure / Environment}}.

## Extra

X before A is increasingly standard: uncontrolled hemorrhage kills faster than airway problems in trauma. Classic teaching is ABCs; NREMT accepts both. D = AVPU + gross neuro + glucose if AMS. E = expose for hidden injury, then prevent hypothermia.
