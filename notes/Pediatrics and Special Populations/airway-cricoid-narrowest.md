## Text

In pediatric patients, the narrowest point of the airway below the vocal cords is the {{c1::cricoid cartilage::structure}}; in adults the narrowest point is the {{c2::glottis::structure}}.

## Extra

This anatomical difference is why uncuffed ET tubes were traditionally used in young children — the cricoid provided a natural seal. Many modern pediatric protocols now use cuffed tubes with careful pressure monitoring.
