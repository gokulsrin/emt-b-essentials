## Text

The pediatric <b>epiglottis</b> is {{c1::floppier / more omega-shaped::characteristic}} compared to the adult epiglottis, which is why a <u>straight (Miller) blade</u> is preferred — it allows {{c2::direct lifting of the epiglottis::technique}}.

## Extra

The pediatric trachea is also shorter, raising the risk of inadvertent right mainstem intubation. The floppy epiglottis is the key anatomical driver of blade-choice preference in infants.
