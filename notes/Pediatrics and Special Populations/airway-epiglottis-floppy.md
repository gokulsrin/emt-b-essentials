## Text

The pediatric <b>epiglottis</b> is {{c1::floppier (more omega-shaped)::characteristic}} and the trachea is {{c2::shorter::characteristic}}, making it easier to inadvertently intubate the {{c3::right mainstem bronchus::structure}}.

## Extra

The floppy epiglottis is one reason a straight (Miller) laryngoscope blade is often preferred in infants — it allows direct lifting of the epiglottis.
