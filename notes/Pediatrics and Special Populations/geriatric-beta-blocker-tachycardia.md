## Text

<b>Beta-blockers</b> blunt the <u>tachycardic response</u> in shock — a "normal" heart rate in an elderly patient on beta-blockers {{c1::does not rule out::interpretation}} hemodynamic compromise.

## Extra

Always note current medications when assessing an elderly patient. A resting HR of 70 bpm in a beta-blocked patient in early shock may look reassuring but is falsely normal.
