## Text

In an infant (< 1 year), the pulse is checked at the <u>brachial artery</u> — located on the {{c1::medial upper arm::location}}, between the biceps and triceps — because the infant's {{c2::short, fat neck::anatomic reason}} makes the carotid difficult to palpate reliably.

## Extra

In children 1 year and older, use the carotid (or femoral) like an adult. Brachial is specifically the infant CPR pulse-check site per AHA guidelines. Take no more than 10 seconds to check; if HR < 60 with poor perfusion or no pulse → start CPR.
