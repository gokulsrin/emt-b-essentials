## Text

Pediatric trauma differences from adults: proportionally {{c1::larger / heavier head::anatomic}} → more intracranial injury; {{c2::larger body surface area–to–mass ratio::anatomic}} → greater heat / fluid loss; children {{c2::compensate well then decompensate rapidly::physiology}}.

## Extra

The pediatric head is the most common site of significant injury in blunt trauma. Because children compensate so effectively, hypotension is a very late and pre-terminal sign — normal BP does not equal adequate perfusion in a pediatric trauma patient.
