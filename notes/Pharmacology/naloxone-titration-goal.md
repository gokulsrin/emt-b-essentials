## Text

<b>Naloxone</b> titration goal is {{c1::adequate spontaneous respirations::goal}}, <u>not</u> full alertness — over-reversal precipitates abrupt {{c2::opioid withdrawal (vomiting, agitation, acute pulmonary edema)::consequence}}.

## Extra

A responsive but groggy patient with a respiratory rate of 12–14/min is the target endpoint. If you fully reverse a chronic opioid user, the precipitated withdrawal can include vomiting with aspiration risk, combativeness that endangers the crew, and rarely acute pulmonary edema.
