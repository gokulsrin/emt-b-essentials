## Text

<b>Nitroglycerin</b> acts as a {{c1::venous vasodilator::mechanism}} → decreased <u>preload</u> → decreased myocardial oxygen demand.

## Extra

Nitroglycerin also causes some arterial dilation and direct coronary vasodilation. The primary benefit in ACS is preload reduction (venodilation), which decreases the work the ischemic heart must do. In pulmonary edema, venodilation offloads the left ventricle.
