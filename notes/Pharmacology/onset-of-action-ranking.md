## Text

Onset of action by route — fastest: {{c1::IO / IV::routes}} → middle: {{c1::IM / SL / IN::routes}} → slowest: {{c1::PO / transdermal::routes}}.

## Extra

IO achieves plasma levels equivalent to IV because the marrow sinusoids drain directly into venous circulation. SL and IN bypass first-pass metabolism. Transdermal onset can be hours to days (e.g., nitroglycerin patches for prevention, not acute treatment).
