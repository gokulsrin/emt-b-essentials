## Text

<b>Oral glucose</b> is indicated for hypoglycemia when the patient is {{c1::alert with an intact gag/swallow reflex::patient condition}}.

## Extra

Blood glucose threshold is typically less than 60–70 mg/dL per local protocol. Never give oral glucose to an unconscious or obtunded patient — aspiration risk is high and the gel can occlude the airway. If the patient improves, transport anyway — hypoglycemic events recur, especially with long-acting sulfonylureas.
