## Text

<b>Routes of administration</b> an EMT must recognize: {{c1::PO (oral)}}, {{c1::SL (sublingual)}}, {{c1::IM (intramuscular)}}, {{c1::IV (intravenous)}}, {{c1::IO (intraosseous)}}, {{c1::IN (intranasal)}}, {{c1::INH (inhalation)}}, {{c1::transdermal}}, {{c1::SC (subcutaneous)}}, {{c1::rectal}}.

## Extra

EMT-B scope primarily uses PO, SL, IM (auto-injector), IN, and INH. IV and IO are ALS routes; SC and rectal are rarely used at the BLS level but appear on NREMT.
