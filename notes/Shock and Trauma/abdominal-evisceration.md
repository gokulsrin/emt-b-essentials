## Text

For an <b>abdominal evisceration</b>: <u>do NOT replace</u> the protruding organs; cover with {{c1::sterile gauze moistened with sterile saline::primary dressing}}, then seal with an {{c1::occlusive (non-adherent) outer dressing::outer dressing}}, and position the patient {{c2::supine with knees flexed::position}} to reduce abdominal wall tension.

## Extra

Replacing organs introduces contamination and can damage exposed bowel. Moist saline gauze prevents the bowel from drying out and adhering; the occlusive outer layer retains moisture and warmth. Flexing the knees relaxes the rectus abdominis, decreasing intra-abdominal pressure and the urge to push organs back through the wound. Treat for shock, high-flow O₂, rapid transport — these are time-critical surgical patients.
