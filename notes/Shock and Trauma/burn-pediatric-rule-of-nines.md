## Text

In the pediatric <b>Rule of Nines</b>, the head is {{c1::larger (~18%)::size and %}} and each leg is {{c2::smaller (~14%)::size and %}} compared to the adult values.

## Extra

Pediatric body proportions shift with age: infants have disproportionately large heads and smaller lower extremities. Standard adult Rule of Nines underestimates head burns and overestimates leg burns in children. The Lund-Browder chart is more precise but not required at the EMT level.
