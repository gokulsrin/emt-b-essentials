## Text

Early signs of <b>compensated shock</b> (BP still normal):
{{c1::tachycardia}}, {{c1::tachypnea}}, {{c1::anxiety/restlessness}}, {{c1::cool pale clammy skin}}, {{c1::delayed capillary refill (> 2 s)}}, {{c1::narrowing pulse pressure}}.

## Extra

The body maintains systolic BP by increasing heart rate, increasing peripheral vascular resistance (vasoconstriction), and redistributing flow away from the skin and gut. Normal BP in the face of significant mechanism should not be reassuring — treat on mental status and skin signs.
