## Text

<b>Crush syndrome</b>: prolonged compression releases {{c1::myoglobin::protein}} from necrotic muscle, causing <u>rhabdomyolysis</u> and <u>hyperkalemia</u>; treat with aggressive {{c2::IV fluid resuscitation::treatment}} and monitor for {{c2::cardiac arrest::complication}}.

## Extra

Hyperkalemia from rhabdomyolysis can cause fatal arrhythmias upon extrication. Some protocols call for IV fluid initiation before release of the crushing force. Transport immediately after extrication.
