## Text

Late signs of <b>decompensated shock</b> (<u>compensation has failed</u>):
{{c1::hypotension (SBP < 90)}}, {{c1::altered mental status / decreased LOC}}, {{c1::weak or absent peripheral pulses}}, {{c1::cyanosis}}, {{c1::bradycardia and bradypnea}}.

## Extra

Bradycardia + bradypnea in a shock patient is a pre-arrest rhythm. Hypotension is a late sign — an adult has typically lost roughly 30% of blood volume before BP drops. Pediatric patients compensate even more vigorously and crash precipitously.
