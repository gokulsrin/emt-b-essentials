## Text

<b>Hypovolemic shock</b> subtypes: {{c1::hemorrhagic::blood-loss type}} (internal or external bleeding) vs {{c2::non-hemorrhagic::non-blood-loss type}} (burns, dehydration, vomiting, diarrhea).

## Extra

Both subtypes reduce preload via fluid loss. Non-hemorrhagic hypovolemia causes plasma loss rather than whole-blood loss; the approach is similar but fluid type differs at the hospital level.
