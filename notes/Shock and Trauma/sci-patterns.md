## Text

<b>Central cord syndrome</b>: {{c1::upper extremities weaker than lower::deficit pattern}}.<br><b>Anterior cord syndrome</b>: {{c2::motor loss + pain/temp loss; vibration/proprioception preserved::deficit pattern}}.<br><b>Brown-Séquard syndrome</b>: {{c3::ipsilateral motor loss, contralateral pain/temp loss::deficit pattern}}.

## Extra

Central cord is the most common incomplete SCI, often from hyperextension in elderly patients with cervical stenosis. Brown-Séquard results from hemisection (stab wound). Anterior cord is associated with flexion-compression injuries.
